DESCRIPTION ABOUT GREEK GODS
GODS DESCRIPTION WEAPON
Hera The goddess of marriage Scepter capped with a pomegranate and a cuckoo
Aphrodite The goddess of beauty, sensuality and love. Her Roman equivalent is Venus. Girdle-like belt called a cestus as her weapon to increase her female attraction.
Athena She is the goddess of war, civilization, wisdom, reason, intelligence, combat strategy, victory, science, crafts, industry, inventions, arts, crafts, navigation, heroes , strength, courage, protection, city state, education, justice, law and skill. Spear, a shield, and a helmet.
Hermes He is the Olympic messenger god, of borders and travelers who cross them, of wit and commerce in general, of cunning, of thieves and liars, and the one who guides souls in the underworld, Hades. Sword of gold
Apollo He is described as the god of the arts, of the bow and arrow, who threatened or protected from the heights of the heavens, being identified with the light of truth. A bow and a lyre
Artemis She is the Hellenic goddess of hunting, wild animals, virgin land, births, virginity and maidens, who brought and alleviated women's diseases. Bow and arrow
Hephaestus He is the god of the forge and fire, as well as blacksmiths, craftsmen, sculptors, metals and metallurgy. Smith's hammer, anvil, and a pair of tongs.
